Overview

Timecards

Lightspeed's online timecards are a paperless, electronic alternative to traditional paper timecards.  Time is reported and submitted using a mobile phone, tablet, or laptop.  The Lightspeed timecard system includes the following features:

 

·         Crew Members can enter time on their mobile phones

·         Department Approvers can review and approve the timecards on mobile devices or laptops

·         Payroll Accountants can review the submitted hours and, using online worksheets, derive a final pay breakdown

·         Production Accountants and UPMs can review and approve the final timecards

·         Timecards can be instantly compared to online Production Reports

Start Forms

In order to use electronic timecards, employees must have valid start forms.  Start forms are created via the Cast & Crew List.

Box Rental & Mileage Forms

Each electronic timecard includes a Box/Kit Rental Form and a Mileage Form.  These forms may be filled out and submitted with the timecards.  Box rental and mileage amounts are automatically transferred onto the timecard.

Timecard Approval and Status

When timecards are submitted by an employee, the timecards are reviewed and approved by timecard Approvers via an Approver Hierarchy. The Approver Hierarchy is the order in which timecard Approvers review and approve their timecards.  The Approver Hierarchy is created on the Payroll Preferences screen.

 

Each timecard has a status, which conveys where in the timecard approval process it is.  Timecards start with a status of Not Submitted and, after all time has been recorded, submitted, and approved, finish with a status of Locked, Approved.  Each status is represented by a timecard icon, visible on the Approver Dashboard.

 

A timecard will have one of the following statuses:

 

·          Not Submitted

Created timecards that have not been submitted.

 

·         Submitted

A submitted timecard has one of three possible statuses based on where it is in the approval chain relative to the user.  Each status is designed to provide contextual information for a specific user.  For example, a submitted timecard may be simultaneously approved by Approver A and ready for review by Approver B.

 

The various Submitted statuses are:

o        Submitted, Final Approval Pending

This is shown to users who have already submitted or approved the timecard, or who have no approval responsibility for the timecard.

 

o        Submitted, Awaiting Initial Approval(s)

This is shown to users who have a responsibility to review and approve the timecard, but who are waiting on others to approve it before them.

 

o        Submitted, Ready For Your Review

This is shown to users when a timecard is in their approval queue and is awaiting their review and approval.

 

·         Rejected

A timecard is Rejected when it is sent down the approval chain to a user, by an approver, because it requires corrections or edits.

 

There are two Rejected statuses:

o        Rejected

This is shown to users when a timecard has been rejected and has not yet been resubmitted.

 

o        Rejected, Ready For Your Review

This is shown to users who have had the timecard rejected directly back to them, and they have not yet resubmitted it.

 

·         Recalled

A timecard is Recalled when an approver needs to edit a timecard they had already approved.  The timecard is then pulled from the queue of an approver higher in the approval chain.

 

There are two Recalled statuses:

o        Recalled

This is shown to users when a timecard has been recalled and has not yet been reapproved.

 

o        Recalled, Ready For Your Review

This is shown to the user who has recalled a timecard and has not yet reapproved it.

 

·         Resubmitted

A timecard is Resubmitted when it has either been rejected or recalled and then has subsequently been edited and resubmitted for approval.

 

The various Resubmitted statuses are:

o        Resubmitted, Final Approval Pending

This is shown to users who have already submitted or approved the timecard, or who have no approval responsibility for the timecard.

 

o        Resubmitted, Awaiting Initial Approval(s)

This is shown to users who have a responsibility to review and approve the timecard, but who are waiting on others to approve it before them.

 

o        Resubmitted, Ready For Your Review

This is shown to users when a timecard is in their approval queue and is awaiting their review and approval.

 

·          Approved

This is shown when the timecard has been approved by the final approver.

 

·          Locked, Approved

This is shown when the timecard has been locked by the payroll department.  The timecard is permanently in read-only mode.

 

·          Void

This is shown when the timecard has been voided by an approver. These are not paid.

Electronic Signatures

As a timecard is submitted and approved, it gathers electronic signatures.  These signatures are appended to the bottom of the timecard as part of the digital audit trail.  A signature is required when a timecard is submitted or approved.  A signature is not required when a timecard is rejected or recalled.

 

Signatures are collected via a dual-credential identity verification process.  When an employee electronically signs a timecard, the employee must provide their Lightspeed password and PIN.

 

Each electronic signature is time stamped and assigned a unique identifier (UID).
